This demonstration uses Akka remote actors to fetch N-gram source files produced from 6% of all published English language literature as of 2009. The N-gram source files are then processed into indices and stored on worker nodes.

While processing, the head node will accept a seed word as input and generate a Markov Chain of following words, selecting them from the indexed data weighted by occurrence frequency in the corpus. A chain is built by appending successive words provided by worker nodes until a maximum word chain limit is reached. The process is then repeated with a new word.

When a remote actor is queried to provide a list of following words given an antecedent, it processes a local copy of English language literary texts and returns a the list of words along with the frequency of their occurrence in the corpus. The head node selects a following word at random but weighted by the frequency of occurence of the following word.
